MedTrack

Background

This project was completed from July to August 2025 for a virtual summer class by a team of four people. We chose to find ways to support personal healthcare. As this was an online class, all communication was virtual, mostly via a group chat, so keeping tabs on everyone’s progress could be challenging, though everyone was committed to the work.

Research

Our research began with each team member conducting an interview with someone who manages their own health and medications, or, someone who manages someone else's medications. Two interview subjects were young adults who took medication themselves, one was an elderly woman who managed her own medication, and one was a woman who managed her own medication, along with the medications of her in-laws and children. In all, we had four interviews, we wrote summaries of each interview, along with a list of key takeaways.

Synthesis

After conducting our interviews, we all analyzed our own and each other’s interviews, and came up with the list of needs:
  • Reminders to take medication on time.
  • Record of what medication has already been taken.
  • Help with navigating insurance, such as explaining how much, if any, of the cost of medication is covered, and how to contact them if there is an issue.
  • Specific instructions on how medication should be taken, such as for more complicated processes like injections. Instructions should also be provided for potential alternative methods for taking medication, as some people will be unable to take medications in the traditional way. This could include instructions on breaking up pills for those who struggle to take pills.
  • Options to have other people, approved by the patient, review what medications have been taken. This can be especially useful for parents with memory issues who may need others to ensure they are taking their medications at the right time.

We then had four team members make a storyboard and write a paragraph clarifying the storyboard, and one team member edit the paragraphs and storyboards for the sake of clarity. This process helped us determine what features should be included in our final prototype.

Design Iterations

Basing our prototype on a paper prototype I made, we fleshed out the design in our first iteration of our prototype. We each made certain pages and provided feedback and edits on pages others made. We included the following features:
  • A login page
  • Reminders about certain medications that could be marked as taken or not
  • A history of medication that has and has not been taken
  • A page to edit the reminders the user gets regarding medication
  • A page that shows the cost of their medication and the cost after insurance, and allows them to search their coverage
  • A menu page that could take the user to any part of the app
  • A page to sync to their pharmacy
  • A page that would display what, if any, medications their pharmacy had available to them
  • A page that lists all medications the user takes, where they can click on certain medications to learn more details.
We reviewed the prototypes of two other teams in our class and had our work reviewed, leading to us making the following changes
  • Added space to the top of the screen so that text would not be cut off
  • Added a new user page
  • Added a forgot username or password option
  • Added accessibility settings

Final Design

After having our prototype reviewed, we finalized our design and made as many of the suggested adjustments as we could.

Future Changes

In the future, there are further changes that could be implemented. The medication details page lacks descriptions of why medication was prescribed, and has a great deal of text that may be overwhelming to the user. These pages were my responsibility, and I would endeavor to add clarity and simplicity if I had time to make improvements.

Additionally, we do not know much about medicine, being able to consult with medical professionals would help us understand what would have been necessary to have in our design.
